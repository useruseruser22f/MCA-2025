Week 5 – Metadata Schema & MEI Enrichment

In this lab I focused on the metadata for my dataset theme: Frédéric Chopin’s Cantabile in B-flat major, B.84. The aim was to design a small catalogue schema and then encode that information directly into the <meiHead> of my MEI file, so that future users (or software) can reliably understand what piece the file represents.

Task 1 – Metadata Schema

For my music catalogue I created a simple metadata schema that could be reused across all pieces in my dataset. It includes the required elements (title, artist/composer, publisher) plus at least two additional fields:

  • Title – the main title of the work, as it appears on the score.
  • Composer – the creator of the work (here, Frédéric Chopin).
  • Publisher – the organisation that issued the score used for encoding (in this case the IMSLP digital edition).
  • Date of composition – the year the piece was written (1834).
  • Genre / work type – a short term describing the style or category, here recorded as a character piece.
  • Catalogue number – a scholarly identifier for the work, in this case the Brown catalogue number B.84.

Together these elements give enough information to identify the work, situate it historically, and distinguish it from other Chopin pieces that may share similar titles or keys.

Task 2 – Enriching the MEI Header

For this task I opened my Week 3 MEI file in the Verovio Editor and edited the <meiHead> section. I replaced the automatically generated, mostly empty header with one that follows the MEI 5.x guidelines.

In the <titleStmt> I added:

  • The full work title: Cantabile in B-flat major, B.84.
  • A <respStmt> identifying the role Composer and the encoded name <persName>Frédéric Chopin</persName>, linked to a VIAF authority record using the @auth.uri attribute.

In the <pubStmt> I encoded:

  • <publisher> – “IMSLP – International Music Score Library Project”.
  • <pubPlace> – “Paris”.
  • <date isodate="1834">1834</date> – the year of composition.

Finally, in <notesStmt> I summarised the schema in a short annotation, noting the genre (character piece) and catalogue number (B.84). I left the existing <encodingDesc> in place so that it still records that the file was transcoded from MusicXML using Verovio.

After editing, I checked the file in Verovio until there were no red error markers in the header, which indicates that the metadata is valid according to the MEI schema.
