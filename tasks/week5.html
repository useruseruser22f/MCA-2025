Week 5 — Metadata Schema & MEI Enrichment

My Metadata Schema

For my music catalogue, I created a metadata schema with the following elements. This schema ensures that every piece in my dataset — starting with Chopin’s Cantabile in B-flat major, B.84 — can be described consistently.

  • Title – the official title of the piece.
  • Composer – name of the composer, linked to an authority record when possible.
  • Publisher – publisher of the score used for encoding.
  • Date of Composition – year the piece was composed.
  • Genre – musical category.

These five elements satisfy the requirement of having at least five metadata fields, including the three required ones: title, artist, and publisher.
