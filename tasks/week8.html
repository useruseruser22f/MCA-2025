Short discussion (time–frequency vs waveform)

A waveform only shows changes in amplitude over time, so it is useful for visualising loudness and overall envelope, but it does not tell us which pitches or harmonics are present. A log-frequency spectrogram adds the frequency axis, which makes it possible to see the distribution of energy across low, mid and high ranges at each moment in time. For example, in my spectrograms it is much easier to distinguish the left-hand bass notes from the right-hand melody, and to see where the pedal creates overlapping harmonics. This kind of time–frequency view is therefore much more informative for analysing texture, register, and changes in timbre than a waveform alone, even though both are derived from the same audio signal.
